Foxtail Millet Pongal

Nutritional Values:


Calories: 320-250kcal
Protein:9-10 grams
Fat:8-10 grams
Carbohydrates:45-50grams
Fiber:4-5 grams
Cholesterol:10-15 mg(Due to Ghee)

Preparation Time: 10 minutes Cooking Time:30 minutes Total Time:40 minutes Servings:2

Ingredients:

Foxtail Millet: ½ cup
Yellow Moong Dal: ¼ cup
Water: 3 cups
Ghee (clarified butter): 2 tablespoons
Cumin Seeds: 1 teaspoon
Black Peppercorns: ½ teaspoon
Ginger (grated): 1 teaspoon
Green Chilies: 1-2, slit (optional)
Curry Leaves: 10-12
Cashew Nuts: 8-10, halved
Asafoetida (hing): a pinch
Salt: to taste

Preparation Method:

Roast the millet and dal:
Dry roast the foxtail millet and moong dal separately in a pan over medium heat for about 2-3 minutes until aromatic. Wash them thoroughly under running water.

Cook millet and dal:
In a pressure cooker, add the washed foxtail millet and moong dal along with 3 cups of water and a pinch of salt. Cook for 3-4 whistles or until both the millet and dal are soft and well cooked. Allow the pressure to release naturally.

Temper the spices:
In a small pan, heat the ghee. Add cumin seeds, black peppercorns, and cashew nuts, and sauté until they turn golden brown. Add grated ginger, curry leaves, green chilies (if using), and a pinch of asafoetida. Sauté for another 1-2 minutes until the ginger releases its aroma.

Mix and simmer:
Open the pressure cooker and mash the cooked millet and dal slightly.
Add the prepared tempering into the millet-dal mixture.
Simmer for 3-5 minutes, adjusting salt and consistency with a little hot water if needed.

Serve hot:
Garnish with some additional ghee if desired and serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

Little Millet Upma

Nutritional Values:


Calories:170-200kcal
Protein: 5-6 grams
Fat:2-3 grams
Saturated Fat: 0.5g, Omega-6-fatty acids:1g
Carbohydrates:30-35g
Fiber:4-5grams
Cholesterol:0mg

Preparation Time: 15 minutes Cooking Time:20-25 minutes Total Time:35-40 minutes

Ingredients:

Little Millet: 1 cup
Water: 2 cups
Ghee (clarified butter)/Oil: 1 tablespoon
Onion(Finely Chopped): 1
Carrot, grated(Optional) : 1
GreenPeas :As per your need
Mustard Seeds: 1 teaspoon
Green Chilies: 1-2, slit
Curry Leaves: 10-12
Chopped Fresh Cilantro(For Garnish) Asafoetida (hing): a pinch
Salt: to taste

Preparation Method:

Rinse the millet and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.

Heat ghee or oil in a large pan over medium heat.

Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.

Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.

Add chopped onion, carrot, and Greenpeas (if using). Saute until the vegetables are tender.

Add chopped green chilies and saute for another minute.

Add 2 cups of water and bring to a boil.

Add the soaked and drained millet. Stir well.

Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 15-20 minutes or until the millet is cooked and fluffy. Season with salt to taste.

Garnish with chopped cilantro.

Tips: Adjust water ratio according to millet type (1:1.5 for Kodo, 1:2 for Little).
Don't overcook; millet should be fluffy, not mushy.

Millet Biscuits

Nutritional Values:


Calories:120-150kcal
Protein: 2-3 grams
Fat:2-3 grams
Sodium:50-100mg
Fiber:2-3grams

Preparation Time: 15minutes Cooking Time:15-20 minutes Total Time:35-40 minutes Servings:2

Ingredients:

Millet Flour: 2 cups(Any Variety)
Whole Wheat Flour:1/2 cup
Sugar:1/4 Cup
Butter/Oil: 1/4 cup unsalted,Melted
Baking Powder:1/2 tsp
Vanilla Extract:1/2 tsp
Milk:If required
Optional:Nuts(Walnuts, Almonds), Seeds(Sesame, Sunflower), Dried Fruits(Cranberrie,raisins) Salt: A pinch

Preparation Method:

Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
Mix dry ingredients (millets, wheat flour, sugar, baking powder, salt).
Add melted butter, vanilla extract, and optional ingredients. Mix until combined.
Gradually add milk to form a dough.
Roll out dough (1/4 inch thickness).
Cut into desired shapes.
Bake 15-20 minutes or until lightly golden.

Tips:

Use different millet varieties for unique flavors.
Add spices (cinnamon, ginger) or herbs (basil, rosemary).
Replace sugar with honey or maple syrup.
Use coconut oil or ghee for added flavor.
Experiment with various nuts or seeds.

Millet Pancake

Nutritional Values:


Calories: 250-300
Protein: 5-6grams
Fat: 8-10grams
Sodium: 200-250grams
Fiber: 4-5grams

Preparation Time: 15minutes Cooking Time:15-20 minutes Total Time:35-40 minutes Servings:2

Ingredients:

Millet Flour: 1 cups(Any Variety)
All-purpose Flour:1/2 cup
Sugar: 2 tablespoons sugar
Butter/Oil: 2 tablespoons
Baking Powder:2 tsp
Milk: 1 cup (Dairy or non-diary eg. almond milk)
Optional:Nuts(Walnuts, Almonds), Seeds(Sesame, Sunflower), Dried Fruits(Cranberrie,raisins), chocolate chips
Salt: A pinch

Preparation Method:

In a large bowl, whisk together millet flour, all purpose flour, sugar, baking powder, and Salt
In a seperate bowl, whisk together milk, egg, and melted butter or Oil.
Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients and stir until combined. The batter should still be slightly lumpy.
If desired, add blueberries, chocolate chips, or nuts and fold into the batter.
Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat.
Drop the batter by 1/4 cupfuls onto the skillet or griddle.
Cook for 2-3 minutes or until bubbles appear on the surface and edges start to dry.
Flip and coook for another 1-2 minutes or until golden brown
Serve warm with your favorite toppings(eg. Maple syrup, honey, fresh fruit, whipped cream)

Tips:

Use a combination of millet and all-purpose flour for better texture.
Adjust the amount of milk to achieve the desired consistency.
You can also add other ingredients like cinnamon, vanilla extract or chopped herbs for extra flavor.
Foxtail millet: slightly sweet and nutty flavor
Porso millet: mild, slightly sweet flavor
Finger Millet: nutty, slightly sweet flavor

Coconut flour
Rice flour
Corn flour

Millet Chips

Nutritional Values:


Calories: 320-250kcal
Protein:9-10 grams
Fat:8-10 grams
Carbohydrates:45-50grams
Fiber:4-5 grams
Cholesterol:10-15 mg(Due to Ghee)

Preparation Time: 10 minutes Cooking Time:30 minutes Total Time:40 minutes Servings:2

Ingredients:

Foxtail/Finger or Mix of both Millet: ½ cup
Rice flour: 1/4(for crispiness)
Sesame Seeds: 1 tablespoon
Turmeric Powder:1/4 teaspoon
Cumin seeds :1/2 teaspoon
Red chilli powder-1/2 teaspoon
Ajwain:1/4 teaspoon
Oil:1 tablespoon (for dough)
Salt to taste
Water as needed for kneading
Oil for frying or brushing if baking

Preparation Method:

Prepare the Dough:
In a large bowl, combine the millet flour, rice flour, sesame seeds, turmeric powder, cumin seeds, red chili powder, ajwain (if using), and salt.
Add 1 tablespoon of oil and mix the dry ingredients well.
Gradually add water and knead into a stiff, smooth dough. Be cautious not to add too much water at once; add a little at a time until the dough is firm.

Roll and Cut the Chips:

Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a thin sheet (about 1/8 inch thick) on a clean surface. You can lightly dust the surface with rice flour if needed to prevent sticking.
Use a cookie cutter or a knife to cut the dough into desired chip shapes (triangles, circles, squares, etc.)

Fry or Bake the Chips:

For Frying: Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Once hot, fry the chips in batches until golden and crisp. Make sure not to overcrowd the pan. Remove and place on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
For Baking: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Place the chips on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Lightly brush with oil and bake for 12-15 minutes, flipping halfway through, until crispy and golden.

Cool and Serve:

Once fried or baked, let the chips cool completely before serving. This will help them become crispier.

Serve hot:

Serving Suggestions:

Serve the millet chips with your favorite dip like hummus, salsa, or yogurt dip.
You can store the chips in an airtight container for up to a week.

Millet Laddu

Nutritional Values:


Calories:120-140kcal
Protein:2-3 grams
Fat:7 - 8grams
Saturated Fat:4-5 g
Sodium :1-2 mg
Potassium :100-150mg
Carbohydrates:15-18g
Iron:10-15% of the DV
Vitamin B1(Thiamin): 10-15% of the DV

Preparation Time: 15 minutes Cooking Time:10-15 minutes Total Time:20-30 minutes

Ingredients:

Millet flour(foxtail, porso, or little millet ) :2 cups
Grated jaggery :1 cup
Ghee :1/2 cup
Cardamom Powder:1/4 tsp
Pinch of Salt

Preparation Method:

Roast millet flour in a pan until fragrant.
In a seperate pan, melt ghee and jaggery.
Add cardamom powder and salt.
Mix roasted millet flour with jaggery mixture.
Add chopped nuts(if using).
Shape into small laddus.
Let cool and store.
